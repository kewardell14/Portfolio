Project Overview

This project focuses on redesigning the packaging for a single product across three distinct audiences: kids ages 7–10, college students, and seniors 65+. Through empathic research and visual exploration, I identified how age, lifestyle, and usability needs influence packaging expectations.

I developed three audience-specific packaging solutions that align structure, typography, and messaging while remaining functional as three-dimensional objects.

Client:

Kinder, Hershey's, and Pepperidge Farms

Disciplines:

Branding, Digital, Packaging, Social Media, Photography

Kids, Ages 7-10

While kids never need a reason to want ice cream sandwiches, kids should feel that this package is special. The kid's packaging takes on the brand of Kinder, so keeping with Kinder's brand recognition of playful surprises, the packaging focuses on showcasing the unique chocolate center.

College Students

The college student's packaging assumes the brand of Hershey's because Hershey's is more well known for its versatility and popularity. This package caters to college students in need of a break or some self-indulgence, while maintaining a modest, and literal design.

Seniors, Ages 65+

Targeting specifically grandparents, this package focuses on bringing the connecting experience and feeling of a real malt shop or ice cream parlor. This vintage package encourages memorable experiences and carrying the tradition of getting a sweet treat from your grandparents.
